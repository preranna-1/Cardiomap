Emergency Awareness

Know the Warning Signs

Every second counts. Recognizing symptoms early can save a life.

Heart Attack

Myocardial Infarction — Act within 90 minutes

  • 1
    Chest Pain or Pressure — Tightness, squeezing or crushing in the chest lasting more than a few minutes
  • 2
    Radiating Pain — Discomfort spreading to left arm, shoulder, neck, back or jaw
  • 3
    Shortness of Breath — Difficulty breathing even at rest, with or without chest pain
  • 4
    Cold Sweat or Nausea — Breaking into a cold sweat, feeling sick or lightheaded suddenly
  • 5
    Sudden Extreme Fatigue — Unusual tiredness that comes on suddenly, especially in women
If you suspect a heart attack:Call emergency immediately. Do not drive yourself. Chew aspirin if available and not allergic.

Stroke — Use F.A.S.T.

Every minute = 2 million neurons lost. Act immediately.

  • F
    Face Drooping — One side of the face droops or feels numb. Ask the person to smile — is it uneven?
  • A
    Arm Weakness — One arm is weak or numb. Ask to raise both arms — does one drift down?
  • S
    Speech Difficulty — Speech is slurred, strange or impossible. Ask to repeat a simple sentence.
  • T
    Time to Call — If ANY of these signs appear, call emergency immediately. Note the exact time symptoms started.
  • +
    Sudden Severe Headache — The worst headache of your life, or sudden vision loss in one or both eyes
Golden Window:Clot-busting treatment must start within 4.5 hours of stroke onset. Time is brain — act immediately.
